Comparison of Passive and Active Data Collection Methods
Category Passive Data Collection Active Data Collection
Description Occurs automatically as users interact with digital devices and apps. Users don't have to do anything—data is collected in the background. Occurs when users deliberately enter information or respond to requests.
Examples
  • Step counts from connected fitness trackers
  • Sleep data from wearables
  • Voice patterns
  • Typing patterns
  • Symptom logs
  • Medication records
  • Survey responses
Benefits
  • Requires minimal user effort
  • Can capture subtle changes that active reporting might miss
  • Can capture information that may not be detectable in passive monitoring, such as subjective experience
Considerations
  • Privacy
  • Relies on user engagement
