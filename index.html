Class Description Regulatory requirements
Class I Low-risk devices with minimal potential to cause harm to patients; general controls are sufficient to ensure safety and efficacy. E.g., bandages, stethoscopes, disposable gloves Must follow basic regulatory requirements that apply to all medical devices, such as proper labeling and manufacturing standards, and registration with the FDA
Class II Moderate-risk devices that require special controls to ensure safety and efficacy; present greater risk than Class I. E.g., powered wheelchairs, pregnancy test kits, etc. Requires rigorous evidence of safety and efficacy. If a new device is shown to be equivalent to an already-approved device, no new testing is necessary.
Class III High-risk devices, often life-sustaining or life-supporting, with significant potential to cause serious harm or death if they fail. E.g., implantable pacemakers, orthopedic implants Requires extensive clinical trial data to demonstrate safety and efficacy.
