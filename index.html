Statement Purpose Key Components Connection to Others

Statement of Financial Position

Sometimes referred to as the Balance Sheet

Shows what the organization owns, owes, and its net assets

Gives a snapshot of the finances at the end of the fiscal year

 Assets, Liabilities, Net Assets Total Net assets is also Net Assets, end of year on the Statement of Activities; Cash is also Cash, end of year on the Cash Flow Statement
Statement of Cash Flows Explains how cash moved in and out of the organization during the fiscal year Operating, Investing, and Financing Activities; Beginning & Ending Cash Balances Tells the story of where the Cash, end of year total came from, which is shown as Cash on the Balance Sheet

Statement of Activities

Sometimes referred to as the Operating Statement or Income Statement

 Summarizes revenues and expenses; shows change in net assets during the fiscal year Revenues, Expenses, Change in Net Assets Tells the story of where Net Assets, end of year came from, which is shown as Total Net Assets on the Balance Sheet; Total expenses is also Total expenses on the Statement of Functional Expenses
Statement of Functional Expenses Breaks down expenses by function (program vs. admin/fundraising) and type over the fiscal year Expense Categories by Function (e.g., salaries, rent, supplies) Total expenses match those on the Statement of Activities

Notes to the Financial Statements

Sometimes referred to as the Footnotes or Notes

 Provides detailed explanations and context for the numbers in the financial statements Accounting policies, breakdowns of line items, commitments, contingencies, restrictions Supports all four statements by clarifying assumptions, definitions, and financial details
