Benefits of Hospital-Based Care Limitations of Hospital-Based Care
  • 24/7 professional monitoring offers continuous symptom and pain management
  • immediate access to medications and procedures like antibiotics, surgeries, and other urgent care
  • immediate access to advanced tools and equipment like MRIs, ventilators, and X-ray
  • access to subspecialists, such as cardiologists and neurologists in a centralized location
  • immediate possibility for advanced surgical interventions that cannot be performed elsewhere
  • disruption of routine can cause confusion, discomfort, and delirium, often in elderly patients
  • fragmented communication across providers can negatively impact care
  • increased potential for infections like sepsis, the number one cause of death in hospital patients
  • high costs of hospital stays, procedures, and staffing can create personal and public stressors
  • discharge and transitions to home care or rehabilitation centers can be poorly coordinated
