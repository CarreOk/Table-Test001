Companies developing health care solutions, especially for-profit companies, often design solutions primarily with individuals from high income countries in mind. They often assume easy internet and technology access, that all users are able to bear some cost, or that most users speak the language their solution is written in.

If their solution improves outcomes among their users, this kind of thinking can widen health care inequities between higher income and lower income groups, even while improving outcomes overall.

To avoid this outcome, solutions developers need to bear in mind that mitigating as many potential barriers to their solution as possible can expand their audience and promote their solution’s adoption—a benefit for any digital solution.

Additionally, policy makers must remember that factors that don’t seem related to health care can in fact have a big impact on it. Policies to promote equitable solutions could

  • Provide incentives for developers to translate their app into different languages
  • Encourage developers to price on a sliding scale based on income
  • Facilitate access to assistive devices for those who need them
  • Support the development and availability of affordable, robust technology infrastructure for all
